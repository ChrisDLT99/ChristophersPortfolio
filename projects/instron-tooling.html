Lab Fixtures Designs

R&D Automation • 2025

Overview

My primary objective was to improve overall laboratory efficiency through the development of specialized test fixtures. The image above highlights a 4-point bend alignment tool, custom Instron attachments, and an adjustable mounting assembly engineered to integrate with the Instron load cell.

Problem

A key challenge in the development of these tools was maintaining tight dimensional tolerances, necessitating several design revisions to accommodate machining constraints and mitigate potential failure during fabrication.

Approach

  • Concept exploration and trade studies
  • CAD modeling / simulation / FEA
  • Rapid prototyping and test plan
  • DFM review with vendors

Results & Impact

I designed custom Instron attachments that interfaced directly with the load cell, removing the need for secondary mounting components. Since no standard components existed for these test configurations, the purpose-built tooling substantially increased test efficiency, accuracy, and setup consistency.

Reflection

For future iterations, I intend to increase the number of formal design reviews with team members to leverage peer critique, strengthen design decisions, and elevate overall product quality.
