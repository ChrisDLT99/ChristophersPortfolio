Push'N Cushion — Project

Project Management • 2022

Overview

Me and my Team designed and developed a mechanical seat-lift for elderly and mobility-impaired users, optimizing lift geometry and load distribution to minimize joint stress while enhancing user stability, comfort, and functional independence.

Problem

A major constraint of the project was limited material availability caused by COVID-related supply chain disruptions. This required a complete redesign of the final prototype, transitioning from the originally specified plastic components to a wood-based construction while preserving functional performance. This challenge ultimately strengthened the design by requiring rapid iteration and material-driven problem solving under real-world constraints.

Approach

  • Research
  • Concept exploration
  • CAD modeling / simulation

Results & Impact

While users with mobility limitations responded positively to the product’s performance, feedback highlighted opportunities to reduce our overall size and weight to improve usability and portability.

Reflection

In future designs, we aim to implement an intelligent sensing feature that automatically recognizes when the user leaves the seat, further enhancing safety, usability, and user experience.
